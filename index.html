FAQ · HOW TO USE
How do I download a video?
Paste the link up top, hit ANALYZE TARGET, choose a quality (or AUDIO ONLY · MP3), then RIP IT. The file saves to the folder shown in SAVE TO — your Downloads folder by default.
I'm only getting low quality — where's HD / 4K? (YouTube)
YouTube hides HD/4K behind a logged-in session. In SESSION COOKIES pick the browser you're signed in to YouTube with (it auto-detects), then ANALYZE again. It reuses your existing cookies locally — nothing leaves your Mac.
  • Chrome / Brave / Edge: works after a one-time macOS "Always Allow" prompt for Safe Storage.
  • Firefox: works with no setup at all.
  • Safari: macOS keeps its cookies private — it needs a one-time Full Disk Access grant for the app (the SESSION COOKIES note has a button that opens the right settings pane). If you'd rather not, just use Chrome/Brave/Edge/Firefox instead.
Where do my downloads go? Can I change it?
Your Mac's Downloads folder by default. Change it in SAVE TO — type a path or click BROWSE…. Playlists and channels get their own subfolder, numbered in order.
Can I download a whole playlist or channel?
Yes, on YouTube. Paste a playlist or channel link and it switches to batch mode: set MAX VIDEOS if you don't want the whole thing, pick one quality, then RIP ALL.
Can I get just the audio (MP3)?
Yes — choose AUDIO ONLY · MP3. It pulls the best audio track and converts it to an MP3.
Which sites can I download from?
YouTube (including Shorts) is fully supported. They work for many public links via the underlying engine, but aren't hardened yet — they keep improving through updates.
Can I use it from my phone or another computer?
Yes, on the same home Wi-Fi. While the app is running it shows a http://192.168.x.x:1337/?key=… address — open that on any device. It's locked to your local network and a secret key, so the public internet can't reach it.
How do updates work?
When a new version is available a gold banner appears at the top — one click installs it and the app restarts itself. You can also hit check for updates in the footer.
Is this legal and safe?
It runs 100% locally and only fetches what you ask for. Use it for videos you own, have permission to download, or that are licensed for it (e.g. Creative Commons). Respect each site's Terms of Service and your local copyright law.
What this app does NOT do
  • It won't download DRM-protected or paid content — Netflix, Disney+, Prime, Apple TV+, Spotify and similar are encrypted and off-limits.
  • It can't grab private, follower-only, or login-required posts unless you supply your own signed-in browser session — and even then, only what your account can already see.
  • It does not crack paywalls or bypass age/region/access controls beyond your own account.
  • It's not a bulk site-scraper — it downloads the links you give it, not whole websites or every user on a platform.
  • It never uploads, shares, or posts anything. No account, no cloud — nothing leaves your Mac.
  • It is not affiliated with YouTube, TikTok, Instagram or Facebook.
A download failed — what can I try?
  • Pick your browser in SESSION COOKIES — this fixes most "403 / forbidden / private" errors.
  • Check the link opens normally in a browser.
  • Sites change often (especially YouTube). Click refresh engine in the footer to update the download engine (yt-dlp) in seconds — this fixes most "it suddenly stopped working" breakage.
  • Live streams still in progress and members-only content generally won't work.
